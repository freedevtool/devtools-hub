📝 Top 10 Grammar Mistakes and How to Fix Them

📅 March 7, 2026 • By DevTools Hub • 6 min read

Good grammar makes you look professional and credible. In fact, 74% of hiring managers say grammar mistakes in applications are immediate red flags. Here are the most common grammar errors and how to avoid them.

1. Their vs There vs They're

❌ Wrong: Their going to the store. There car is blue.
✅ Right: They're going to the store. Their car is blue.

Rule: "They're" = They are | "Their" = possession | "There" = location

2. Your vs You're

❌ Wrong: Your going to love your new job.
✅ Right: You're going to love your new job.

Rule: "You're" = You are | "Your" = belongs to you

3. Its vs It's

❌ Wrong: The dog wagged it's tail. Its a beautiful day.
✅ Right: The dog wagged its tail. It's a beautiful day.

Rule: "It's" = It is | "Its" = belonging to it

4. Then vs Than

❌ Wrong: She is taller then me. First we'll eat than go.
✅ Right: She is taller than me. First we'll eat then go.

Rule: "Then" = time | "Than" = comparison

5. Affect vs Effect

❌ Wrong: The weather will effect our plans.
✅ Right: The weather will affect our plans.

Rule: "Affect" = verb (to influence) | "Effect" = noun (the result)

6. Me vs I

❌ Wrong: Me and John went to the store.
✅ Right: John and I went to the store.

Rule: Remove the other person - "I went to store" (not "Me went")

7. Fewer vs Less

❌ Wrong: I have less books than you.
✅ Right: I have fewer books than you.

Rule: "Fewer" = countable items | "Less" = uncountable amounts

8. Who vs Whom

❌ Wrong: Who did you give it to?
✅ Right: To whom did you give it?

Rule: "Who" = subject (he/she) | "Whom" = object (him/her)

9. Alot vs A lot

❌ Wrong: I have alot of work.
✅ Right: I have a lot of work.

Note: "Alot" isn't a word! Always two words: "a lot"

10. Could of vs Could have

❌ Wrong: I could of done better.
✅ Right: I could have done better.

Rule: "Could have" (could've) not "could of"
